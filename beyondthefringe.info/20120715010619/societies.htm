Societies & Associations

  • AKREP Oriental Rug Society Oriental Rug Society in Gothenburg, Sweden. A society for people interested in textiles, such as rugs,kelims, saddle-bags, sacks and other textiles related to the Orient.
  • American Conference on Oriental Rugs - Association of American rug societies, with conventions every two years.
  • Armenian Rugs Society  Organization devoted to the appreciation of Armenian rugs.
  • The Chicago Oriental Rug and Textile Society - CORTS is a not-for-profit organization established to foster and promote interest in the art of hand-woven carpets and related ethnic textiles.
  • Goteborg Rug Society Rug collectors club in Sweden. In English and Swedish.
  • Hajji Baba Club The oldest American rug collector club, in New York City.
  • International Hajji Baba Society  Collector club, in Washington, D.C.
  • New England Rug Society One of the more active American rug societies. Site includes some on-line exhibitions.
  • Oriental Rug Society of New South Wales - The Oriental Rug Society of NSW Inc is an Australian non-profit voluntary society.
  • Oriental Rug Society in Gothenburg (Sweden) - A non-commercial society for collectors of oriental textiles, includes exhibitions, photos and facts.
  • Pazyryk Gesellschaft.  German collector club, with exibitions and other information.  German and English.
  • San Francisco Bay Area Rug Society - Large, active rug collector club, with on-line exhibitions.
  • Seattle Textile and Rug Society - The Seattle Textile And Rug Society (STARS) was founded in 1998, and is dedicated to increasing knowledge and appreciation of fine rugs and textiles.
  • Teppichfreunde Norddeutschland - An informal association of people who like old and antique rugs, carpets or textiles and who wish to enrich their knowledge in this field.
  • Textile Society of America - The Textile Society of America, Inc. provides an international forum for the exchange and dissemination of information about textiles worldwide, from artistic, cultural, economic, historic, political, social, and technical perspectives.
  • Textile Society of Hong Kong - The Textile Society of Hong Kong aims to explore all aspects of both traditional and contemporary textiles.
  • Turkish American Society of Northeast Ohio An club promoting Turkish culture, including rugs, with regular programs and lectures.
  • One Thousand Years of
    Chinese Textiles
    Exhibition of Chinese textiles, on display in 1995 at the Hong Kong Museum of Art.
