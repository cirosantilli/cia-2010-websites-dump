Las Vegas

Nestled in the heart of The Venetian Resort-Hotel-casino The Grand Canal Shoppes is a luxury shopping center inspired by Venice with cobblestone walkways, illuminated sky ceilings, centered along a quarter-mile long Grand Canal and gondola ride. The center features an authentic replication of St Mark's Square where you will find unparalleled entertainment experiences performed by the Streetmosphere daily. Enjoy over 80 shoppes and restaurants, such as Dooney & Bourke, Movado, Sephora, Kenneth Cole, Wolfgang Puck's Postrio, Canaletto and TAO Asian Bistro & Nightclub.



Madison Avenue

Between 57th Street and 85th Street, Madison Avenue is identified as "the fashionable road". In this area is where most of the very well known fashion designers, jewelers and upper class hair salons are located.

Among the store located in this NYC shopping area are: Anne Fontaine, Barneys New York, Bergdorf Goodman, Calvin Klein, Carolina Herrera, Celine, Cerruti, Chanel Boutique, Chloe, Christian Dior, Donna Karan, Emanuel Ungaro Boutique, Escada, Gianfranco Ferre, Gianni Versace Boutique, Giorgio Armani.

The Forum Shops at Caesars

The Forum Shops at Caesars is a 634,000 sq ft (58,900 m2) luxury mall connected to Caesars Palace on the Las Vegas Strip in Las Vegas, Nevada. Built as an extension wing of the main hotel, this was the first destination mall on The Strip, the mall now fronts on The Strip. It is one of the most successful high-end shopping malls in the United States and the highest grossing mall in America, with higher sales per ft than Rodeo Drive in Beverly Hills. The venue includes more than 160 shops, luxury and haute couture, high fashion boutiques as well as 13 gourmet restaurants from names such as Bobby Flay and Wolfgang Puck.

Mall of America

Mall of America (also MOA, MoA or the Megamall) is a super-regional shopping mall located in the Twin Cities suburb of Bloomington, Minnesota, USA. The mall is located southeast of the junction of Interstate 494 and Minnesota State Highway 77, north of the Minnesota River and is across the interstate from the Minneapolis-St. Paul International Airport. In the United States, it is the second largest enclosed mall in terms of retail space but is largest in terms of total enclosed floor area. Opened in 1992, the mall received 40 million visitors in 2006. Triple Five Group, owned by Canada's Ghermezian family, fully owns and manages the property.
