The History, Where Cricket Started

First played by the English over 300 years ago, cricket quickly expanded into every continent on the globe. The origins of cricket are very vague, and many theories have been put forward suggesting its origins. The English game originated in the sheep-raising country of the South East, where the short grass of the pastures made it possible to bowl or roll  a ball of rags or wool at a target. That target was usually the wicket-gate of the sheep paddock, which was defended with a bat in the form of a shepherd's crooked staff.
