  • Bhutan ’s Military

    The Royal Bhutan Army is Bhutan's military service. It includes the Royal Bodyguard and the Royal Bhutan Police. Membership is voluntary. The standing army numbers about 6,000 and is trained by the Indian Army. Bhutan handles all of its foreign affairs, having diplomatic relations with 22 countries. Indian and Bhutanese citizens may travel to each other's countries without a passport or visa using their national identity cards instead. Bhutanese citizens may also work in India without legal restriction. Bhutan does not have formal diplomatic ties with its northern neighbor, China, although exchanges of visits at various levels between the two have significantly increased in the recent past. Bhutan’s border with China is largely not demarcated and thus disputed in some places.
