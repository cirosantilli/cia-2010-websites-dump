Sun join hands with Intel

January 22, 2007 | Leave a Comment

For years, Sun Microsystems has been engaged in intense competition with Intel in the server chip space. Sun owned the SPARC microprocessor and used it in its server products. However, since the dot com bust, Sun has lost a lot of market share. To capture lost market share, Sun now allows its Operating System Solaris to run on AMD’s x86 processor. This has resulted in AMD capturing server processor market share from Intel.

Now, Intel has reached an agreement with Sun that would allow customers to run Solaris on Intel’s x86 processors. According to the deal, Sun will be using Xeon based processors on some of its products. Still no word on Itanium. Looks like Intel is not having too many successes with the Itanium processor. But, still Intel should be extremely pleased with this announcement since they are now positioned very well both in the consumer and server processor market to take back lost market share from AMD, which continues to lag behind Intel in benchmarks. AMD now should be hoping the release of its new quadcore processor codenamed “Barcelona” will improve its fortunes.

Airbus A380 finally wires up…

January 21, 2007 | Leave a Comment

After Vista, and PS3 the one other product that has been delayed for a long time has been Airbus’s A380 super jumbo jet. One of the biggest hurdles that was faced by Airbus has been problems with on-board electrical installation. By now it seems that EADS (parent company of Airbus) has finally finised the electrical installations and so now the jumbo jet seems to be on track for delivery to Singapore airlines.

EADS must be relieved by the progress because it has suffered huge financial losses due to the A380 project. It has also resulted in BAE system, the co-partner of EADS in the Airbus project, to pull out. Plus the delays in the project has resulted in heavy job losses and market share losses to Boeing, whose latest airplanes has much better fuel efficiency than Airbus. Singapore airlines which has been waiting for A380 to replace its aging fleet must also be revealed by this latest news. So now passengers can expect to ride a A380 flight fairly soon

Vista is now downloadable!

January 19, 2007 | Leave a Comment

I once read a great blog post by Mark Lucovsky, a former engineer at Microsoft and currently employed by Google, on his opinion about ‘Shipping Software‘. He correctly pointed out Microsoft has forgotten how to ship its software. He used the example of the need to ship the .NET run-time environment to run any .NET applications. While, he was more concerned with applications, I think the same holds true for Operating Systems. Microsoft traditional model of shipping OS using a traditional CD rom or through pre-installation is archaic to say the least.

Now, as a first step for the time ever Microsoft is making is Operating System available for download. It’s far easier for end-users. Microsoft has also developed the download process to resume on dropped connection. I hope their servers are able to withstand the heavy traffic load that they are likely to experience and not have limits like they did on Microsoft’s Office Beta Software. The prices for upgrading to different versions of Vista is also available on their website. I think Microsoft should leverage the Internet to distribute more of its software especially the OS.

Joost!

January 17, 2007 | 1 Comment

Yesterday it was movies and today its TV. Skype founders have launched a new service named Joost (previously codenamed Venice project). The service brings near DVD quality live TV over the internet. The service is still in beta and is expected to be fully available to users in about 6 months time. According to the press,

Chief executive Fredrik de Wahl said Joost would combine the best features of broadcast television - a near-DVD quality and full-screen picture - with the best features of the internet, such as the ability to create personalised channels, chat to like-minded viewers and reschedule programmes on the move. “People are looking for increased choice and flexibility in their TV viewing experience, while the entertainment industry needs to retain control over their content,” said Mr de Wahl. “With Joost, we’ve married that consumer desire with the industry’s interests.”

I encourage readers to check out their website http://www.joost.com/ for more details regarding their service. There are also screen shots available. It also looks very interactive with chat options available along with ability to rank content. It will be really interesting to see how this service evolves and if the Skype founders are once again able to revolutionize TV distribution over the internet just the way they did with Voice Over IP.

Apple juggernaut marches on..

January 17, 2007 | Leave a Comment

Apple Inc stunned investors with another excellent quarter with profits touching the $1 billion mark. The company also posted record revenue for the quarter raking in $7.12 billion. This is traditionally the strongest quarter for Apple since it includes Christmas/Thanksgiving period where there is high demand for electronic gadgets. So expectedly both iPod and Macs were in strong demand. Apple’s market share in the PC business is now at 5 percent. Thanks still well behind leader Dell which has about 34 percent market share. However, Apple has carved a nice little niche market for itself.

However, on the downside Apple’s forecast of revenues between $4.8-4.9 billion for the next quarter has disappointed investors. However, Apple tends to beat its forecast quite comfortably and so I don’t read too much into it. With the upcoming launches of Apple TV and the iPhone expect Apple to be one of the hottest stock in the technology sector.
