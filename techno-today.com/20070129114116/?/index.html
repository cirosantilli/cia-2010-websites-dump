Ad Sharing by YouTube

January 28, 2007 | Leave a Comment

Google invented adSense and revolutionized online advertising. Now it looks like YouTube (which is now owned by Google) is planning to do the same with online videos with a new program which would allow content providers to get a share of profit by hosting videos on its website. This is a fantastic idea for YouTube to make money and its also fantastic for viewers to get great copyrighted videos legally. By doing this, YouTube is moving ahead and legitimizing itself after often being accused of encouraging copyright infringements.

YouTube says it plans to use “video fingerprinting” (I have no idea what this means) to determine if the content is legitimately owned by the person hosting the video. This will of course be challenging but I think as time goes by they should be able to work this one out. Google’s Management should surely be looking at this program with great excitement because they need to recover the $1.65+ billion dollars they spent on purchasing YouTube. But this sure sounds like an exciting proposition…

Is Microsoft’s Live strategy faltering?

January 26, 2007 | Leave a Comment

Microsoft’s biggest foray into online business is faltering. Today, Microsoft was forced to admit that its online business will grow at best by 3-5 percent instead of the earlier 11 percent forecast. More worryingly, Microsoft’s search engine is losing market share to Google and Yahoo. The upcoming launches of Vista, Office, and Exchange is likely to take attention from Live for some time now, but Microsoft seriously needs to do some thinking.

Live.com, which was started at the insistence of Microsoft CTO Ray Ozzie is a move away from MSN. Live.com is more geared towards providing data collection and generation tools (i.e., search engine, feeds, blogs, mail, local etc) to users. The problem Microsoft is facing is its trying to copy Google instead of trying to be unique. The following are some of my suggestion for improving their improving their Live service,

1. Microsoft needs to start small and specialize in areas where Google isn’t good at or where its software is still at its infancy. Prime examples are Blog Search, Groups, Feed Readers, and Videos.

2. Another thing Microsoft has to learn is how to display ads. Microsoft’s ads are very annoying. The banner ads on the top when you login to the new live mail service is pathetic to say the least. They need to find ways to put ads without diminishing the usability of their service.

3. Microsoft needs to purchase smaller companies to fill in areas where its not good at. Google has been very good at this.

4. Another thing Microsoft needs to do is to integrate services to provide users with more unified experience. They need to integrative Live services into their Desktop OS, Mobile OS (I think this is one of the major improvements in the next OS release codenamed ‘Crossbar’).

5. Finally, Microsoft needs to build a network of developers akin to the MSDN network they built for Windows OS. These things are critical to their success and will shape Microsoft’s online strategy.

Earnings Report

January 23, 2007 | Leave a Comment

Corporate Earnings for the latest quarter is out and the results are a mixed bag. Today, tech giants Sun Microsystems, Advanced Micro Devices (AMD), and Yahoo reported their earnings and the results are summarized below,

1. Sun Microsystems - Sun has been on the upswing lately capturing lost market share with its decision to open source their software and adopt x86 processors from AMD, and Intel. Sun’s latest server products and operating system Solaris has been widely welcomed. Moreover, Jonathan Schwartz, chief executive of Sun, also enjoys considerable investor confidence after taking over from its long time CEO Scott McNealy. Overall, Sun earned $126 million on sales of $3.6 billion.

2. Advanced Micro Devices (AMD) - Things couldn’t be worse for AMD. It’s engaged in a severe price war with Intel. Intel after establishing a clear lead with its latest 65nm process, has been making deals with the likes of Sun Microsystems to see its x86 based server products like the Xeon. AMD was also hit by a one time charge for the purchase of ATI. AMD lost $574 million dollars on sales of $1.77 billion.

3. Yahoo! - Online giant Yahoo looks to be on the upswing again. It CEO Semel said that Project Panama which is the next generation search ads software will be ahead of schedule. This reverses his previous quarter report that the project is likely to be delayed by about 3 months. Yahoo hopes to gain more revenue from search with the upcoming software. Yahoo’s current quarter was however not very positive. The company recorded sales of $1.23 billion.

Sun join hands with Intel

January 22, 2007 | Leave a Comment

For years, Sun Microsystems has been engaged in intense competition with Intel in the server chip space. Sun owned the SPARC microprocessor and used it in its server products. However, since the dot com bust, Sun has lost a lot of market share. To capture lost market share, Sun now allows its Operating System Solaris to run on AMD’s x86 processor. This has resulted in AMD capturing server processor market share from Intel.

Now, Intel has reached an agreement with Sun that would allow customers to run Solaris on Intel’s x86 processors. According to the deal, Sun will be using Xeon based processors on some of its products. Still no word on Itanium. Looks like Intel is not having too many successes with the Itanium processor. But, still Intel should be extremely pleased with this announcement since they are now positioned very well both in the consumer and server processor market to take back lost market share from AMD, which continues to lag behind Intel in benchmarks. AMD now should be hoping the release of its new quadcore processor codenamed “Barcelona” will improve its fortunes.

Airbus A380 finally wires up…

January 21, 2007 | Leave a Comment

After Vista, and PS3 the one other product that has been delayed for a long time has been Airbus’s A380 super jumbo jet. One of the biggest hurdles that was faced by Airbus has been problems with on-board electrical installation. By now it seems that EADS (parent company of Airbus) has finally finised the electrical installations and so now the jumbo jet seems to be on track for delivery to Singapore airlines.

EADS must be relieved by the progress because it has suffered huge financial losses due to the A380 project. It has also resulted in BAE system, the co-partner of EADS in the Airbus project, to pull out. Plus the delays in the project has resulted in heavy job losses and market share losses to Boeing, whose latest airplanes has much better fuel efficiency than Airbus. Singapore airlines which has been waiting for A380 to replace its aging fleet must also be relieved by this latest news. So now passengers can expect to ride a A380 flight fairly soon
