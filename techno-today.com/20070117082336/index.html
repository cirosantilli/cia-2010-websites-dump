HP invents!

January 16, 2007 | Leave a Comment

The research labs at Hewlett-Packard has developed a technology that would make FPGA chips far more efficient. FPGA or Field Programmable Gate Arrays are programmable chips that can be used to implement customized logic through the use of Hardware Description Language (HDL). Over the last few years FPGA chips have become very attractive solution since the cost of custom ASIC designs have sky rocked due to increased verification and mask costs. However, FPGA(s) does not provide the performance of ASIC designs. Another problem with FPGA chip is the huge amount of area that is taken up by wire density. Only a small amount of chip area is taken up by CLB which are the programmable logic units.

HP has now invented new technique uses an architecture called “field programmable nanowire interconnect (FPNI)” which replaces the current signal routing with a crossbar made up of super thin 15nm wires which sits on a layer above the silicon-based transistors to route the signals.

The expense of fabricating chips is increasing dramatically with the demands of increasing manufacturing tolerances,” said Snider, senior architect, Quantum Science Research, HP Labs. “We believe this approach could increase the usable device density of FPGAs by a factor of eight, using tolerances that are no greater than those required of today’s devices.”

Is Streaming Movies the next big ‘killer’ Internet service?

January 16, 2007 | Leave a Comment

I have been waiting for this for many many years and I am sure many of you were as well. With the growth of broadband the ability to stream high quality movies to end users has become possible. Surprising not many companies have been able to deliver this service. But, the momentum has picked up. Netflix, which is a leading company that distributes movies to end users through mailing DVDs, has decided to enter this market. They now have limited movies to be able to be streamed to end users. The best part of this package for users is that existing and new members of their service don’t have to pay any extra subscription fees.

The Netflix streaming movie service is built on top of Microsoft’s software. For the moment at least, it is available for Windows users only. Further more, High-Definition is not available. But for starters this looks extremely promising. One thing that would be good for services such as these is an option to be able to stream movies to the TV instead of just the computer. There were some prototypes, and products at the recently concluded CES that also demoed similar service. But, I predict this will indeed be a killer application in the next few years. Now, I wonder what Blockbuster is going to do to respond to this initiative of Netflix .

Web Search Market Share Report

January 15, 2007 | Leave a Comment

Both Google and Yahoo have gained a little market share during the month of December at the expense of Microsoft and Ask.com according to web audience measurement firm comScore Networks Inc. Google’s share is now 47.3 percent with a gain of 0.4 percent, while Yahoo’s share of the market rose 0.3 percent to 28.5 percent in December over November, the survey found. The biggest looser is Microsoft Corp. whose share slid 0.5 percent to 10.5 percent. Ask.com’s market share also dropped a little to 5.4 percent.

According to the report the number of U.S. Web search queries has grown 30 percent since December of 2005, comScore said. Consumers performed 3.2 billion searches on Google sites, while Yahoo drew 1.9 billion searches.

Article’s Source: Reuters

Microsoft snaps Donald Ferguson

January 15, 2007 | Leave a Comment

Donald Ferguson, who was the chief architect of the WebSphere platform, has been hired by Microsoft. He becomes a Microsoft Technical Fellow in Platforms and Strategy, in the Office of the CTO, Ray Ozzie. Previously at IBM, Donald Ferguson provided overall technical leadership for WebSphere, Tivoli, DB2, Rational and Lotus products. After continuously losing several employees to the likes of Google, Microsoft has finally been able to pouch a prized technological thinker.

Donald Ferguson has been a leading proponent of XML based Web Services and SOA architectures. This move of his to Microsoft gives Ray Ozzie a boost within the organization since he himself has been championing many of these architectures within Microsoft. However, for IBM and the WebSphere platform this represents a significant loss.

Firefox product roadmap

January 14, 2007 | Leave a Comment

For most of us, Firefox remains the most popular choice of browser. It has popularized features like tabbed browsing, and RSS feeds. It has also supported the use of standards like CSS, and SVG. So its definitely interesting to know whats in the pipeline with respect to new features and improvements to the browser platform. The next major release of Firefox is version 3.0. This is more of an upgrade to version 2.0 in many respects. Mozilla Foundation admits that it would take more time for the code base for version 2.0 to stabilize. The 12 mandatory features for Firefox 2.0 are mostly enhancements to existing features like,

1. Improve Add-On install experience
2. Improve Add-On configuration experience
3. Improve Add-On management experience
4. Provide an extensible bookmarks back-end platform
5. Improve content handling
6. Robust plugin support for Flash, Acrobat, WMV, Quicktime, Java
7. Improved Printing Support
8. Provide MSI install bundle (Windows only)
9. Improve Password Manager, add identity management
10. Security / Privacy Context UI redesign
11. Airbag
12. Improve the user support environment from beginning to end

The remaining P2 (Highly Desired), and P3 (Nice to Have) features can be found on Firefox website. Personally, I was a bit disappointed with version 2.0 both in terms of feature set as well as performance. It looks like version 3.0 is more of the same which is again disappointing. Version 3.0 is expected to be released before the end of the year. However, after this version 4.0 is expected to be a major redesign of the platform with new features. This release is expected sometime in 2008. Although, its too early in the stage to know if the roadmap is adhered to. Until version 4.0, expect no radical or exciting changes on your Firefox browser.
